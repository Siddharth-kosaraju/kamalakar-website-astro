Did you know? The ICMR-INDIAB study found that 81.2% of Indians have some form of abnormal cholesterol levels. High cholesterol has NO symptoms — it silently damages your arteries until a heart attack or stroke strikes.

Even vegetarians can have high cholesterol. The Indian diet is often high in refined carbohydrates, cooking oils, and trans fats — all of which raise bad cholesterol.

Simple steps to manage cholesterol:
- Replace deep-fried snacks with steamed or grilled options
- Use mustard oil or olive oil instead of refined oils
- Include oats, ragi, walnuts, and flaxseeds in your diet
- Eat at least 5 servings of fruits and vegetables daily
- Get a lipid profile test — especially after age 35

A simple blood test can detect cholesterol problems early. Don't wait for symptoms that may never come.
