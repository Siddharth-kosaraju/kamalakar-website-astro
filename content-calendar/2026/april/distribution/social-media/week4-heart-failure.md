This week (April 27 – May 3) is Heart Failure Awareness Week.

Let's clear up the biggest misconception first: Heart failure does NOT mean your heart has stopped working.

It means your heart muscle has become too weak to pump blood as efficiently as it should. Think of it like a pump that has lost strength — it's still running, but not pushing enough blood to meet your body's needs.

The early signs are often mistaken for "getting old" or "being unfit":
- Breathlessness, especially when lying down
- Swollen ankles or feet
- Unusual fatigue that doesn't improve with rest
- Persistent cough or wheezing
- Rapid or irregular heartbeat

The good news? With early diagnosis, proper medication, and lifestyle changes, many patients with heart failure live active, fulfilling lives.

A 2D Echo test can measure your heart's pumping strength and detect heart failure early.

If you recognise these signs in yourself or a loved one, don't ignore them.
