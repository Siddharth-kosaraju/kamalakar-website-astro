## Facebook Post
**Publish:** Tuesday, 2026-04-15
**Format:** Long-form post with engagement question

Summer is here, and Guntur temperatures are already crossing 40°C.

But did you know that extreme heat doesn't just make you uncomfortable — it forces your heart to work significantly harder?

When it's hot, your body sends more blood to the skin surface to cool down. This means your heart beats faster and your blood pressure changes. For people with existing heart conditions, hypertension, or those on heart medications, this can be dangerous.

Here's what you can do:
- Stay hydrated with water, coconut water, and buttermilk
- Avoid going outdoors between 12 PM and 5 PM
- Never skip your heart medications
- Watch for warning signs: dizziness, chest tightness, rapid heartbeat
