## When Should You Consult a Cardiologist?

Many heart conditions develop silently over years before presenting with sudden, life-threatening symptoms. The American Heart Association recommends [cardiovascular risk assessment beginning at age 20](https://www.heart.org/en/healthy-living/healthy-lifestyle/prevention-screening-guidelines), with formal screenings every 4–6 years if risk factors are normal — and earlier or more frequent checks for those with diabetes, hypertension, or a family history of heart disease, all common in the Indian population.

Consult a cardiologist in Guntur if you experience:

- **Chest pain or discomfort** — especially during physical activity or emotional stress
- **Shortness of breath** — during mild exertion or while lying flat
- **High blood pressure** — consistently elevated readings above 140/90 mmHg
- **Irregular heartbeat** — palpitations, skipped beats, or a racing pulse
- **Dizziness or fainting** — unexplained episodes that may indicate a rhythm disorder or valve problem
- **Family history of heart disease** — if a parent or sibling had a heart attack before age 55 (men) or 65 (women)

**Early consultation can prevent serious complications like heart attack.** Do not wait for symptoms to become severe. A simple [ECG, 2D Echo, and TMT](/services/ecg-echo/) can reveal hidden problems and give you peace of mind.

### What is the difference between an angiogram and angioplasty?

A coronary angiogram is a diagnostic procedure — it uses contrast dye and X-ray imaging to visualise the heart's arteries and identify any blockages. An [angioplasty](/services/angioplasty/) is a therapeutic procedure — if the angiogram reveals significant blockages, a balloon-tipped catheter is used to open the narrowed artery and a stent is placed to keep it open. Both procedures are performed in the Cath Lab, and in many cases, angioplasty is done immediately after the angiogram in the same session.

### How often should I get a heart checkup?

The American Heart Association recommends that adults begin cardiovascular risk assessment at age 20, with regular screenings every 4–6 years if risk factors are normal. However, given that Indians face a higher genetic predisposition to heart disease, annual checkups after age 35 are advisable — especially if you have diabetes, hypertension, high cholesterol, a family history of heart disease, or a sedentary lifestyle. A routine [ECG, 2D Echo, and TMT](/services/ecg-echo/) can detect hidden problems early.

### Can heart disease be prevented?

Yes, to a significant extent. The WHO estimates that **over 80% of premature heart attacks and strokes are preventable** through lifestyle modifications. Eating a balanced diet, exercising regularly, maintaining a healthy weight, avoiding tobacco, and controlling blood pressure, blood sugar, and cholesterol levels can dramatically reduce your risk. However, genetic factors and age-related changes mean that regular monitoring by a qualified [cardiologist](/about/) remains essential even for those with a healthy lifestyle.

### When should I visit a cardiologist instead of a general physician?

While general physicians manage common conditions, a cardiologist is needed when symptoms suggest a heart-specific problem — chest pain during exertion, unexplained breathlessness, palpitations, fainting episodes, or abnormal results on an ECG or blood test. If you have already been diagnosed with hypertension, diabetes, or high cholesterol, periodic evaluation by a cardiologist helps prevent complications like [heart failure](/services/heart-failure/) or heart attack. For any cardiac emergency, seek [emergency cardiac care](/services/emergency-cardiac-care/) immediately.
